# Employee-Attrition-Analysis
This project contains an analysis of an HR dataset to answer the business question of "Is employee attrition higher among employees who work overtime compared to those who do not?" which determines what factors are most associated with employee attrition using Microsoft Excel and Microsoft Power BI.

1) Overview:
The goal of this analysis project is to determine which factors specifically working overtime influence employee attrition the most, for the purpose of keeping employees for the longest, and
reducing the costs and efforts of hiring and training of the new employees. 

2) Data Description: 
- Source:” Employee Attrition” - Prashant Patel on Kaggle https://www.kaggle.com/datasets/patelprashant/employee-attrition
- Rows: 1470
- Used Fields: Attrition, Department, Employee Number, Environment Satisfaction, Job Satisfaction, Overtime, Relationship Satisfaction.

3) Tools Used:
- Microsoft Excel: Data cleaning
- Microsoft Power BI: trend identification, data visualization and DAX measures

4) Data Cleaning:
Checked for any duplicate rows, spelling errors or inconsistent formats

5) Analysis:
- former employees vs total employees
- Overtime vs regular-hour former employees
- number of employees by department
- overtime-workers by department (percentage)
- attrition by department (percentage)
- Job, environment, and relationship satisfaction scores of former employees in R&D

6) Key Insights:
- Working overtime increases job attrition by 15.45%
- 56.12% of all attrition was in the R&D department
- 28.57% - 32.33% of former R&D employees were dissatisfied with their job, relationships and environment in the workplace which suggests the reason for their resignation or termination
